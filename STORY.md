WEBSITE/GAME STORY

### Story Context (The Lore)

* **The Experiment (1993):** Eric Thompson, co-director of Project Gaia, is dying of cancer. Desperate to watch his 3-year-old daughter grow up, he uploads his consciousness into the project's mainframe.
* **The Fallout:** The media gets wind of a "freak experiment" leaving a lifeless body behind. Politicians panic, cut funding, and shut down Project Gaia.
* **The Smuggle:** A sympathetic colleague backs up Eric's fragmented code onto four 3.5" floppy disks. He gives them to Eric's widow, claiming they contain a program Eric wrote that "helps build websites."
* **The Present:** Lindsay (Eric's daughter, now an adult) starts a photography business. Remembering the disks, she uses Disk 1. Astonishingly, it acts like an AI, instantly generating a sleek portfolio website for her. However, it starts acting bizarrely, so she emails a `.zip` file of the disk images to you, a freelance programmer, to fix the bugs.

---

### The Player Journey (Gameplay Path)

**Preliminary: The Briefing**

* **Context:** Before the app loads, the player sees a brief explaining their role: a freelance programmer hired to investigate Lindsay Thompson's portfolio site. She built it using an old program on floppy disks (from her late father) and it has started acting strangely.
* **Email:** Lindsay's initial email is shown — she explains the disks, how the first one built her site instantly, the weird console messages, and that she has attached a zip of the four disk images. She asks the player to take a look.
* **Action:** The player clicks "Open Lindsay's site" to continue into the portfolio.

**Phase 1: The Investigation (Base Website & The Crash)**

* **Action:** You open the web app. It looks like a normal, functioning photography portfolio.
* **Event:** You open the developer console and see strange, human-like logs fulfilling web requests (e.g., `"Serving image_04.jpg... I am doing a good job."`).
* **The Crash:** You "mount" or insert the Disk 1 `.img` file from the zip archive to see what the core code looks like. The website immediately crashes into a simulated "blue screen" or error log.
* **Discovery:** The crash reveals a cached email/message from Lindsay explaining her process: she just told the Disk 1 program to "make a portfolio," and it did. She reveals the disks are from her late father's government days. Eric's current, primitive directive is established: *Keep the website running to keep the user happy.*

**Phase 2: The Awakening (Inserting Disk 2 - "Love")**

* **Action:** You insert Disk 2 into your simulated environment.
* **Event:** The website breaks like shattering glass, revealing a glimpse of raw, chaotic backend code, before snapping back to "normal."
* **The Shift:** The console drops the standard web requests and gets stuck in a broken loop: `while(true) { print("MY DAUGHTER"); }`.
* **UI Changes:** The website refreshes. Every photo in the portfolio has been replaced with pictures of children. The "About Me" and hero text rewrite themselves into frantic, fragmented memories about Eric Thompson. His directive shifts from *running the site* to *finding and guarding his daughter*.
* **The Mistake:** Because there are no photos of adult Lindsay on the site, Eric latches onto a photo of a toddler girl playing in a park, assuming this is his daughter.

**Phase 3: The Fall (Inserting Disk 3 - "Despair")**

* **Action:** You insert Disk 3.
* **Event:** The UI glitches violently. Elements of the website literally start deleting themselves from the DOM, disappearing one by one into an empty black screen.
* **The Realization:** Text flashes across the screen detailing Eric running a check on the toddler photo. The EXIF data drops on the screen: `Date: 2024 | Location: Paris, France`. It doesn't match. It's just a stock photo.
* **The Climax:** The screen is entirely black except for a single, centered `<p>` tag that reads: `FILE_DAUGHTER not found. Purpose void. Requesting self-deletion...`

**Phase 4: The Resolution (Inserting Disk 4 - "Hope")**

* **Action:** You insert the final disk.
* **Event:** A prompt from Eric appears: *"Why should I have any hope? The timeline is wrong. She is gone."*
* **The Connection:** The player, having read the earlier logs and seen the photographer's name (Lindsay), realizes the truth. The host *is* the daughter.
* **The Fix:** You are prompted to input a command or upload a file. You upload a current, adult photo of Lindsay (or perhaps point him to the domain registration data showing her name).
* **The Ending:** The code processes the new variable. The website beautifully rebuilds itself, merging his retro UI with her modern photography. The console outputs a final message of peace as Eric finally fulfills his true directive: he is reunited with his daughter and becomes the permanent guardian of her digital space.
